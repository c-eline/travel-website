Interesting Facts about Countries in Latin America

Colombia, the Emerald King:

Colombia reigns supreme in the emerald world, producing over 90% of the world's emeralds. These precious gems come from the lush Andean mountains and have been prized for centuries.


Angel Falls, Venezuela's Cascading Giant:

Nestled in the heart of Canaima National Park, Venezuela boasts Angel Falls, the world's tallest uninterrupted waterfall. Plunging a staggering 3,280 feet (almost 1 kilometer!), the falls create a breathtaking spectacle and mist-filled environment.


The Arid Atacama: Driest Non-Polar Desert:

Spanning Chile and Peru, the Atacama Desert holds the title of the driest non-polar desert on Earth. Some areas receive virtually no rainfall, creating a unique landscape of salt flats, geysers, and otherworldly rock formations.


Bolivia, a Landlocked High Achiever:

Despite having no coastline, Bolivia holds the distinction of being the highest, geographically locked country in the world. La Paz, its administrative capital, sits at a staggering 11,942 feet (3,640 meters) above sea level.


Mexico City, Sinking Giant:

Mexico City, a sprawling metropolis with a rich history, is slowly sinking due to groundwater extraction. The city subsides by an average of around 10 inches (25 centimeters) per year.


The Amazon Rainforest:

Covers an enormous 6.7 million square kilometres, spanning 8 countries in South America and representing an area twice the size of India
